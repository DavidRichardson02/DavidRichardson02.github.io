About Me

I’m a Computer Engineering student with a strong foundation in digital systems, embedded hardware, high-performance C/C++ programming, and numerical modeling. I’m particularly motivated by the interplay of physics, mathematics, and computer science, and I enjoy building systems that unify rigorous theory with practical implementation.

My projects range from FPGA-based real-time signal control and telemetry, to spatial data structure–driven simulation engines, to robust C++ data analysis pipelines. I tend to work bottom-up: understanding constraints at the hardware, algorithm, and numerical levels, then designing clean architectures and interfaces around them.

I’m especially interested in roles and projects involving:

  • Embedded and FPGA-based systems
  • High-performance numerical simulation and modeling
  • Signal processing, control, and real-time telemetry
  • Data analysis, visualization, and tool-building

Long term, I want to work on complex, interdisciplinary systems where careful engineering, mathematical insight, and physical intuition all matter—whether in research labs, R&D teams, or advanced product development.
